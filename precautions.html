PRECAUTIONS  PRECAUTIONS  PRECAUTIONS 

Everyone gets excited when getting ready to build their new machine. However, there are some safety measures and tips to be taken. Here is a list of some of the most important ones.

  • Make sure that you have a spacious area to work with. Opening up packages and laying them out takes more space than one might think.
  • Make sure not to make contact with any pins or the golden interconnects. This could cause a variety of problems from the dirt or oil you may have on your hands.
  • If you are on carpet or on a rug, ground yourself using an anti-static wrist wrap, as not doing so can create shock and damage a highly conductive component. Alternatively, you can use a wooden table and floor.
  • Be prepared with some basic tools like a phillips-head screwdriver and some velcro straps to keep your cables separate and tidy. Some zip ties and wire cutters can help too
  • I highly suggest using PCPartpicker to help you choose the ideal parts for your build. It shows you parts compatibility and different retail prices, among other things.
  • Get yourself some thermal compound
  • If you have no experience or don't feel confident enough on your own, make sure to follow a trusted guide or help from a knowledgeable friend. You don't want to screw your first build, but nowadays almost everyone can build one so it shouldn't be too much of a problem.
